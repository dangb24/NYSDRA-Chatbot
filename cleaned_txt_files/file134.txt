The following Rules shall govern cases which qualify for Alternative Dispute Resolution through presumptive mediation. The Rules set forth herein are intended to apply in the Commercial Division of the Supreme Court of County These Rules supplement and shall be applied in tandem with the Rules of the Alternative Dispute Resolution Program for the Ninth Judicial District (the JD Capitalized terms to the ex tent not defined herein are as defined in the Ninth J D Rules. Rule 1 Program The Commercial Division of the Supreme Court of the State of New York, County of employ s the Alternative Dispute Resolution Program instituted by the Ninth Judicial District. Unless otherwise directed by the Court, cases referred to the Program shall be mediated. Cases which qualify for referral to the Program shall be those cases that have been designated by the Commercial Division as qualify for presumptive mediation, as identified in Rule 2 herein, as well as any cases where all parties have elected to pursue mediation, and any cases where the Court has ex its discretion to refer the cases to mediation. Rule 2 Cases Qualifying f or Presumptive Mediation and Determination of Suitability: Listed below are those cases which have been designated as qualify for presumptive mediation. All such cases shall be referred to mediation utilizing the procedures and forms established by the Ninth JD Rules. Those forms enable parties or their counsel to identify whether their case qualifies for mediation following which the Commercial Division will review such forms to confirm that the case does qualify in which event the mediation shall be initiated and an Order of Reference issued in accordance with the Ninth JD Rules. The following cases are deemed to qualify for presumptive mediation: -1- (1) corporate internal governance disputes and shareholder derivative actions where the entity is closely held and non-public and there is an ongoing business or personal relationship among the parties; (2) employ competition (whether or not employ (3) commercial real estate not involving Yellowstone injunctions; (4) business trans actions involving commercial banks or other financial institutions or lenders not involving an instrument for the pay of money only (5) commercial insurance coverage or environmental insurance coverage; (6) construction litigation; (7) cases where the amount at issue is less than double the jurisdictional threshold for Commercial Division cases pending in W County Supreme Court; (8) any case which does not fall into the list of cases above, but where all parties request mediation, or where the Court directs the case to go to mediation in the exercise of the Court’s discretion. P Automatic Disclosures: (i) Unless otherwise directed by the Mediator, at least ten days before the first mediation session, each party shall deliver to the Mediator a copy of that party marked pleading s (including responsive pleading s) together with a memorandum in the form set forth in the Ninth JD Rules.
