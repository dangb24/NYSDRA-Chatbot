Before you use the booklets and forms, we strongly suggest that you give serious thought to using a lawyer for your divorce, even if you believe that your divorce will be your spouse will not oppose the divorce in any way). There can be a lot more to think about than just ending the marriage and filling out court papers. (For example, there may be property to divide between you and your spouse, or you may need an Order of Protection child support and other financial support, among other things.) If you are unsure how to find a lawyer, these resources can help you: Call the New York State Bar Association Lawyer Referral Service (toll free) at 1-800-342-3661; Go to if you cannot afford a lawyer; Visit the court system's website; Contact your local bar association for a referral; If you live in the New York City area, go to the Collaborative Family Law Center to learn if you may be eligible for or free collaborative law services through a pilot program. Ask at any courthouse about lawyer referral services.
